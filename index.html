About Me

I am a dedicated Software Developer/PC Builder currently pursuing a Bachelor's degree (B.Eng) in Computer Engineering (Software Engineering Option) at Toronto Metropolitan University (formerly Ryerson University). Currently seeking new graduate engineering opportunities for Summer/Fall 2025.

I recently had the opportunity of completing a 12-month co-op internship at Advanced Micro Devices (AMD) with the Customer Software Engineering Team as a Stability Operator & Debug Intern. During my 12-month stay, I built, maintained and directed stability test coverage on a test bench of 50+ systems for next-generation products, reduced test kickoff time by revising and creating new scripts, supported graphics-related debug investigations, and significantly enhancing organizational efficiency and knowledge by curating comprehensive training documents for future interns.

My experience with leadership positions such being an instructor and second-in-command to my air cadet squadron has greatly sharpened my critical thinking and problem-solving skills. The leadership challenges and rigorous training I encountered as a cadet has led me to develop innovative side projects that address complex issues in an efficient manner.

My prior work experience, projects, and classes have provided me with expertise in database management, algorithm development, and software testing and quality assurance. I am particularly interested in these areas, as well as embedded systems, machine learning, and artificial intelligence. As a continuous and quick learner, I am always eager to expand my knowledge and skills.

As for my hobbies, I love gaming, PC building, cooking/baking, and listening to music. The whole story of how I fell in love with PC building is too long for this section but I love sharing it with people. Feel free to check out some recommended PC part lists down below!
